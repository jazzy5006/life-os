👋 Hi there!

Thank you so much for taking care of Timber! He's a miniature poodle puppy and a very good boy. This page covers his everyday routine — what he's used to, what he needs, and how to handle the trickier moments. Please don't hesitate to text or call with any questions!

⚡ TL;DR — The Essentials

Here's Timber's normal routine in 30 seconds:

  • 🍽️ 3 meals a day — a scant 1/3 cup of Fromm puppy food each, in the morning (after his walk or play), around 1–2 PM, and 6–8 PM. Feed the meal first (snuffle mat or puzzle feeder is great), then do training after — his food isn't used as training treats. If he's not hungry at lunch, that's fine — just give him extra at dinner.
  • 🐾 Paw wipes every time he comes in from outside. Hold the wipe out and wait for him to come to you (or call him over), then wipe all four paws.
  • 🚽 Outside to potty first thing out of the crate (8:30–9:30 AM), after naps, after meals, and roughly every 2–3 hrs while awake.
  • 💤 1–2 naps a day, 2–3 hrs each. If he's wound up — zoomies, barking, nipping — that's the sign for a crate nap, with a frozen treat.
  • 🦴 Frozen treats (bone broth/carrot/Greek yogurt/peanut butter) live in the freezer — one every time he goes in the crate.
  • 💧 Water picked up 1–2 hrs before bed to help him hold it overnight.
  • 🌙 Crate whenever works for your evening — he can sleep ~9 hrs overnight, no accidents.
  • 🎓 Knows: come (great recall), sit, down, touch (point your finger, he touches it), paw (offer your hand, he gives a paw). Should sit before going through doors — if he doesn't on his own, just ask for one first.
  • 🛋️ Couch: he doesn't get on furniture at home, so he won't ask — but if your pets are allowed up and you're okay with it, he can join on his own. Just please don't invite him.

🍽️ Feeding

🐾 Food: Fromm puppy food

3 meals a day — a scant 1/3 cup each (just barely 1/3 cup, not a full packed scoop). Meal #1 is in the morning after his walk or play. Meals #2 and #3 are around 1–2 PM and 6–8 PM.

How each meal works: feed him his meal first (snuffle mat or puzzle feeder is great for this), then do a quick training session afterward (see 🎓 Commands & Training) — his food isn't used as training treats. If he's not very hungry at lunch, that's totally fine — just give him a bit extra at dinner. If he still seems hungry, it's fine to offer a bit more — he's a growing boy.
Breakfast — after morning walk or play Lunch ~1–2 PM Dinner 6–8 PM
Pick up his water bowl 1–2 hours before bedtime — this helps him hold it overnight. No need to refill until morning.
🎓 Commands & Training

🐾 What he knows

Timber has a solid little vocabulary already — using these words (and matching hand signals) keeps things consistent for him:

  • Come — he has very good recall and comes reliably when called
  • Sit — right hand, palm facing you, pointer finger and thumb touching
  • Down — same hand shape as sit, but palm facing the floor
  • Touch — point your index finger out, he'll touch it with his nose
  • Paw — hold your hand out, he'll offer a paw
A short training session with a handful of treats happens after each meal (not during) — any of the cues above work great. Mark good behaviour with "good!" and reward right away.
He should sit before going through any door — if he doesn't sit on his own, just ask for one before opening it. It'd also be great if he can keep practicing sitting to ask to go outside for potty — if he wanders to the door, ask for a sit before letting him out.

The rhythm: wake → outside → paw wipe → training + meal → walk → awake time → nap (or crate nap if overtired) → repeat. This is what Timber's used to at home — a guide, not a strict timetable. The pattern matters more than exact times.

🏥 Health & Safety

🍯 Just Good to Know

Toy breed puppies can occasionally get a blood sugar dip if a meal is delayed or he gets very overtired — Timber's never had an episode, just worth knowing.

Signs to watch for: wobbly/unsteady, glassy eyes, shivering, unusual lethargy. If you see this: rub a little Karo syrup or honey on his gums and head to the vet — no need to call first, just go and let me know when you can.

For anything else that seems off — vomiting, diarrhea, not eating, very unusual behaviour — please use the contacts above. Go straight to the vet for anything urgent and let me know when you can.

🚽 Potty & Paw Wiping

🌿 Outside, every time

All potty trips are outside. He should go: first thing out of the crate, after naps, after meals, and roughly every 2–3 hrs while he's awake.

  • Give him a few minutes to sniff around and go
  • The moment he goes, praise him
  • Watch for signals: sniffing, circling, suddenly heading for the door

🐾 Paw wipes — every time he comes back in

Hold a wipe out and wait for him to come to you (or call him over), then wipe all four paws before he wanders off. He's used to this as part of coming back inside, so he won't mind. If you skip it sometimes, that's totally fine too — it's just our house preference, not something he needs.

🌙 Overnight

He sleeps in his crate overnight with no accidents and can go about 9 hours. If he's awake and not settling, he should still get an outside trip every 2–3 hrs.

💤 Naps, Crate & Settling

💤 Naps

Timber takes 1–2 naps a day, lasting 2–3 hours each. He won't often settle into a nap on his own — see below for how to help him get there.

😵‍💫 If he won't settle

He has trouble settling on his own. If he's acting crazy and overtired — running around, barking, nipping — that's the sign it's time for a crate nap, even if it's hard to catch the early signs. It's better to crate him before he gets to that point, but it's understandably tricky to spot in time, so don't worry if he's already wound up by the time you notice.

Try this order:

  1. Offer a chew or a snuffle with treats
  2. If that doesn't settle him, crate nap time — with a frozen treat
No need to feel bad about crating him when he's overtired — it's exactly what he needs, and he's very used to it.

🦴 Frozen Treats

He gets a frozen treat every time he goes in the crate — these are made from combinations of bone broth, carrot, Greek yogurt, and peanut butter. They're stored in the freezer (his own — there's nothing else of his in there).

🏠 Crate Timing

You can put him in the crate whenever works for your evening — there's no need to match a specific time. He can sleep about 9 hours overnight and sleeps through with no accidents.
